Your Benefits Rights and Responsibilities

Your responsibilities as an unemployment claimant

You’re responsible for understanding this important information. Don’t reply to this email! We won’t receive it. If you have questions, log into eServices or call the claims center at 800-318-6022.

You must submit weekly claims

You must submit weekly claims For unemployment purposes, a week is Sunday through Saturday. For every week you want to receive benefits, you must:

  • Submit a weekly claim.
  • Look for work (unless we tell you otherwise).
  • Report your gross earnings and number of hours worked, even if you have not been paid yet.

You must submit weekly claims even if you are:

  • Waiting to hear if you are eligible for benefits.
  • Expecting wages and hours to be added to your claim.
  • Appealing a denial of benefits.
  • Claiming a waiting week.
Your job search

Your job search To be eligible to receive unemployment benefits, you must be:

  • Able to work.
  • Available for work.
  • Actively seeking work.

Unless we tell you otherwise, you also must do one of the following activities each week you submit a claim, or you might lose your benefits. You must:

  • Contact at least three employers each and every week; or
  • Go to your local WorkSource office and participate in three approved job search activities; or
  • Complete a combined total of three employer contacts and job search activities.

You must keep a written log of your job search activities.

For more information, read the Handbook for Unemployed Workers at esd.wa.gov/handbook.

To change how you get paid

Log in to eServices if you want to change how you receive your benefit payments. You can choose to get them on a debit card or deposited directly into your bank account.
